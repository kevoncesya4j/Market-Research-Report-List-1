<p><p>The wood repair epoxy market is primarily segmented into two types: liquid and putty versions. The liquid version is designed for penetrating cracks and filling gaps, offering a smooth finish and strong bonding characteristics, making it ideal for detailed repairs. In contrast, the putty version has a thicker consistency, making it suitable for sculpting and reshaping damaged areas, providing a durable and moldable solution. Both types feature excellent adhesion and resistance to moisture, ensuring long-lasting wood repairs.</p></p>
