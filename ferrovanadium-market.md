<p><p>The ferrovanadium market includes several grades based on vanadium content. FeV40 contains approximately 40% vanadium, while FeV50, FeV60, and FeV80 contain around 50%, 60%, and 80% vanadium, respectively. Higher vanadium concentrations enhance the alloy's strength and stability, making FeV80 particularly desirable for high-performance applications. Nitrided ferrovanadium features added nitrogen for improved hardness and wear resistance, expanding its applicability in specialized steel and alloy production. Each grade serves distinct industrial requirements, influencing pricing and demand dynamics.</p></p>
