<p><p>The non-absorbable suture material market is divided into two primary types: synthetic and natural materials. Synthetic non-absorbable sutures, made from polymers like nylon and polypropylene, offer high tensile strength and resistance to infection, making them popular in various surgical applications. Conversely, natural non-absorbable sutures, derived from materials like silk and cotton, provide good handling characteristics but may have a higher risk of infection. Both types are essential for wound closure, with usage depending on specific medical needs and preferences.</p></p>
