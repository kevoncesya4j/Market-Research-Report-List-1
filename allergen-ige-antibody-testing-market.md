<p><p>The Allergen IgE Antibody Testing market features four main types. Skin prick testing involves applying allergens to the skin to observe reactions. Serum-specific IgE testing measures IgE levels in the blood to identify specific allergies. Patch testing assesses delayed allergic reactions by applying allergens to the skin for a prolonged period. In vitro allergen testing evaluates immune responses outside the body, often utilizing blood samples. These methods help diagnose and manage allergic conditions effectively.</p></p>
