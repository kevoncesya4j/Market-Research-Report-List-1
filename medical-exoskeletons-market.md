<p><p>The medical exoskeleton market is categorized into powered and passive exoskeletons. Powered exoskeletons are equipped with actuators and sensors that provide mobility assistance to individuals with mobility impairments, enhancing their strength and facilitating gait training. In contrast, passive exoskeletons rely on mechanical support and ergonomic design to redistribute weight and reduce strain during activities, aiding rehabilitation and improving posture without active power sources. Both types aim to enhance mobility, improve rehabilitation outcomes, and support independent living.</p></p>
