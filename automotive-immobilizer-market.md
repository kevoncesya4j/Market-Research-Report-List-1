<p><p>The automotive immobilizer market is divided into two main types: OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) and aftermarket. OEM immobilizers are integrated into vehicles during manufacturing, ensuring factory standards and compatibility. They enhance security features and are often covered under warranty. In contrast, the aftermarket segment includes immobilizers sold separately, allowing vehicle owners to retrofit or upgrade their systems. Aftermarket options offer varied features and price points, appealing to consumers seeking customized security solutions beyond factory offerings.</p></p>
